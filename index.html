Hi, I'm Badrinath.

I work at the intersection of deep learning, medical imaging, and earth observation. I care about building models that actually help with cancer diagnosis, surgical training, and understanding how people live on the ground using satellite data.

Currently working on digital pathology pipelines, histopathology to MRI simulation, village-level socioeconomic mapping across India, and groundwater sustainability models.

Selected work
A quick snapshot. More details on the Projects page.
Histopathology → MRI Simulation
Digital pathology · 3D geometry · MRI signal simulation
Building a pipeline from WSIs to SVG, STL, and PLY for simulating diffusion MRI signals, to better understand microstructure and cancer appearance across modalities.
Village-level Socioeconomic Mapping
Satellite imagery · Nightlights · Deep learning
Using daytime satellite images and nightlights to estimate relative wealth index for Indian villages (2003, 2011, 2019), and studying how development and stress evolve.
Groundwater Sustainability Models
Aquifer modelling · Time series · India-wide
Modelling temporal trends in well depth for shallow and deep aquifers using CGWB and Atal Jal data, to identify regions under groundwater stress and understand drivers of change.
AI for Medical Skills and Diagnosis
Micro-suturing · Multimodal AI
Exploring CNN and transformer-based models for evaluating neurosurgical training, and multimodal AI for cancer imaging and diagnostics.
